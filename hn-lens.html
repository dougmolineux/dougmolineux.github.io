Privacy-First Design

Built for how you read

HN Lens gives you personalized summaries of Hacker News comment threads through customizable AI personas. All processing happens on your iPhone.

Browse Top Stories

View the latest Hacker News stories with points, comment counts, and author information. Bookmark articles to read later.

Instant Summaries

Generate 2-sentence or 5-sentence summaries of any comment thread. Choose the perspective that matters to you.

Ask Follow-up Questions

Continue the conversation with your selected persona. Dig deeper into specific aspects of the discussion.

Your Personal Library

Save stories you care about and access them anytime. All your bookmarks stay synced locally on your device.

Complete Privacy

All AI processing uses Apple's FoundationModels framework. No data ever leaves your device. No tracking, no analytics.

Fast and Native

Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 2026

Our Privacy Commitment

HN Lens processes all data on your device. We don't collect, store, or transmit any personal information. This isn't just a policy—it's how the app is built.

What We Don't Collect

HN Lens does not collect any information about you:

  • No names, email addresses, or contact information
  • No reading history or usage patterns
  • No analytics or telemetry data
  • No cookies or tracking technologies
  • No data shared with third parties
  • No advertisements or ad tracking

How the App Works

HN Lens uses Apple's FoundationModels framework for on-device AI processing. Here's what happens when you use it:

  • Fetching Stories: Your device connects directly to Hacker News' public API. No intermediary servers involved.
  • AI Processing: Summaries are generated on your iPhone using Apple's on-device language models. Comment text stays on your device.
  • Local Storage: Bookmarks and cached content are stored using SwiftData, Apple's local database framework.
  • No Cloud Services: We don't operate servers that receive or process your data.

Data Stored on Your Device

The following information stays on your device and is never transmitted:

  • Bookmarked stories and saved articles
  • Selected persona preferences
  • Cached story content and generated summaries
  • App settings and user preferences

You control this data. Clear it anytime through Settings or by deleting the app.

Third-Party Services

HN Lens connects to these external services:

  • Hacker News API: Direct connection from your device to fetch public stories and comments. Hacker News may log API requests according to their privacy policy.
  • Apple FoundationModels: On-device AI framework. No data sent to Apple servers—all processing is local.

We don't use analytics services, advertising networks, or other tracking tools.

Children's Privacy

HN Lens doesn't collect information from anyone, including children under 13. The app is designed to be privacy-safe for all ages.

Security

Since data stays on your device, security relies on your iPhone's built-in protections: encryption, device passcode, and biometric authentication.

Changes to This Policy

Updates to this policy will be posted in the app. We can't notify you directly since we don't collect contact information. Check back periodically for changes.

Your Rights

Since we don't collect your data, there's nothing for us to access or delete. You maintain control through:

  • Clearing cache in Settings
  • Managing bookmarks in the Library tab
  • Deleting the app to remove all local data

Contact

Questions about this privacy policy? Contact us through the App Store listing.

Transparency

Our privacy-first approach is built into the app's architecture. All AI processing uses Apple's on-device FoundationModels framework. Your data never leaves your device because the app doesn't have servers to send it to.

Legal Compliance

This app doesn't process personal data as defined by GDPR, CCPA, or similar regulations because no personal information is collected. Public content from Hacker News is accessed directly by your device.
